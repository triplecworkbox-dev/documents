Introductory letter

My name is Aniké Jenneker. I am a Coloured South African currently living in New Zealand, with a life that has spanned South Africa, China, Dubai, Australia, and now Wellington. I am reaching out because I am ready to commit to genuine therapeutic support — and I want to be honest from the beginning, which is something I have not always managed to do.

```

I am carrying a great deal at once: the weight of cultural displacement and identity, significant financial stress including debt collectors, the grief and guilt of a car accident that seriously hurt my mother, a complicated relationship with my father, an unhealthy attachment to a former employer that contributed to leaving my job, and the pressure of building multiple ventures simultaneously while being unemployed. I am also exploring the possibility that I may be neurodivergent.

I process the world better through reading and writing than through unstructured verbal conversation, and I respond well to sensory and experiential approaches. What I need most is a therapist who can hold the full weight and complexity of what I am building and surviving — without asking me to scale down, simplify, or become ordinary. I have a vision for my life that is large and unconventional, and I need a professional who is curious about that rather than cautious of it.

I am asking these questions not to be difficult, but to protect us both from a poor fit. I hope they open a real conversation.

```
Five screening questions
```
01
Complexity & Simultaneity
"Have you worked with clients who are managing multiple simultaneous life crises — financial, relational, identity, and career — rather than a single presenting issue? How do you approach sessions when everything feels equally urgent and nothing can wait?"
What to listen for: comfort with complexity, absence of a rigid treatment structure, willingness to follow the client's lead rather than impose a pathway.
02
Cultural Identity & Migration
"I have lived across South Africa, China, Dubai, Australia, and New Zealand. My identity as a Coloured South African shapes how I experience belonging, otherness, and self-worth in ways that are difficult to categorise. How do you approach layered cultural identity in your work — particularly when a client's background doesn't fit neatly into your own frame of reference?"
What to listen for: genuine curiosity rather than rehearsed inclusivity language. Willingness to be taught. Experience with diaspora or immigration-related identity fracture.
03
Vision, Ambition & Non-Pathologising
"I am currently building multiple ventures simultaneously. This is not a symptom of avoidance — it is central to who I am and how I survive. How do you work with clients whose lives look structurally chaotic from the outside but are driven by a coherent internal vision? And would you ever encourage a client to scale back their ambitions as a therapeutic goal?"
This is the critical filter. The answer to the last question should be a clear no, or at minimum a very thoughtful qualification. A therapist who defaults to "stability" as the goal will not be a safe space for Aniké.
04
Financial Accessibility & Non-Judgment
"I am navigating significant debt and active financial pressure — this is part of what I am in therapy to work through. I want to be transparent about this from the start. How do you approach fee structures for clients in financial hardship, and how do you ensure that money does not become a recurring source of shame or stress within our working relationship?"
What to listen for: concrete answers, not vague reassurance. Sliding scale availability, willingness to agree a sustainable arrangement upfront, and zero judgment in how they discuss it.
05
Communication Style & Neurodivergence
"I process information significantly better through reading and writing than through open-ended verbal conversation. I am also exploring the possibility that I may be neurodivergent. Do you have experience adapting your therapeutic style for clients who find unstructured talking overwhelming? Do you offer any written components — reflections, session summaries, asynchronous check-ins — and how flexible are you with the format of our sessions?"
What to listen for: actual flexibility, not just willingness. Ideally some experience with neurodivergent clients or a sensory/experiential modality. Openness to written communication between sessions.
```
